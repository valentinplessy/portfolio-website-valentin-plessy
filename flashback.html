CHALLENGE

Music player mobile apps are numerous but few are specialised in a specific music era. I was asked to design one for 80s music lovers and define the direction that will work for the branding and digital UI.

You can view the full prototype here.

PROCESS

The design thinking approach guaranteed user-centric solutions throughout the different steps of the project.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS &
USER RESEARCH

I first researched competitor apps. I looked into Spotify, Mixcloud, and Soundcloud. I studied their usability and aesthetics, and gathered ideas for the UI patterns of my project.

Three key functionalities were defined for the app:

  • Create a profile
  • Search for new artists
  • Make a playlist

I had to come up with a few more. There were two obvious ones: personalized suggestions and an interactive player.

I thought of a third one when talking to music enthusiasts. They were frustrated with the lack of artists’ information on music apps. Sure you can always google the artist but, wouldn’t it be swell if you could learn more about their journey, the producers they worked with, anecdotes about records, and whatnot on that very app you are using? I chose to add an expandable biography on the artists’ screen.

With these functionalities in mind, I started ideating and worked on wireframes.

INSPIRATION BOARD & BRANDING

Wireframes, user-flows, and usability tests done, I gathered ideas to define the branding of the app. I wanted it vibrant with the energy of 80s nightlife, the dark side of Heavy Metal, and the bubbly touch of High-Energy and Italo Disco.

The contrasts between bright colours created the fun and neon mood I was looking for. The black and dark purple served these contrasts. Not only did they recall the night but also 80s Sci-Fi imagery.

ICONOGRAPHY

The choice of sharp edges, which recalled 80s angular shapes and fashion, assured the icons’ consistency.

FINAL DESIGNS

Once the UI style guide was achieved, I perfected the logo and finalized my designs into a minimal yet colourful homage to the tubular decade of the 80s!

RETROSPECTIVE

Flashback was my very first design project and it all went pretty smoothly. I’ve learned the importance of keeping users at the center of each decision. Research and testing taught me to never settle for what I think makes sense, and to acknowledge the wide variety of minds and logic surrounding us.

The biggest challenge may have been designing the icons. Making sure they all look consistent is not as easy as it seems. Nevertheless, I deeply enjoyed the process. I also realized how passionate I am about achieving strong branding and turning my solutions into a beautiful, consistent, and intuitive interfaces.
